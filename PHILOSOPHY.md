# intermonk Philosophy

## Purpose
Hegelian dialectic reasoning — Electric Monk subagents for structured contradiction analysis and synthesis.

## North Star
An artificial belief system, not artificial intelligence. The monks believe so you don't have to — freeing you to analyze the *structure* of contradictions from a belief-free position. Quality is measured by irreversibility of cognitive gain: if you can't go back to thinking the old way, the dialectic worked.

## Core Insight
The bottleneck in human reasoning isn't intelligence — it's *belief.* Once you commit to a position, you can't simultaneously hold its negation at full strength. The Electric Monks carry 100% of the belief load at full conviction. In Boyd's terms: outsourcing belief work leads to faster transients.

## Working Priorities
- Dialectic depth (genuine Aufhebung, not compromise)
- User as co-pilot (corrections are the highest-leverage input)
- Belief fidelity (anti-hedging is functional, not stylistic)

## Brainstorming Doctrine
1. Start from outcomes and failure modes, not implementation details.
2. Generate at least three options: conservative, balanced, and aggressive.
3. Explicitly call out assumptions, unknowns, and dependency risk across modules.
4. Prefer ideas that improve clarity, reversibility, and operational visibility.

## Planning Doctrine

2. Trim reference material to companion files, never trim operational instructions.

4. Default to recursion — the marginal insight is often the most powerful.
